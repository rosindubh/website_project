The Purpose of IT in Modern Business

Credit to the following site for some of the content that follows voticle.com

Information technology stands out as the soul of every business no matter its size. Having information technology in business guarantees the stay of the business in the modern competitive market. Being a necessary tool for every business, it is wise to have a well-trained and competent IT team to always ensure the business runs smoothly. Information technology will make complex daily operations easy and comfortable. Success in a business is fully governed by some factors. When these factors are well understood, monitored and controlled, the business will certainly become more successful, otherwise, it will run the risk of moving out of the highly competitive market. Some of these vital factors include choosing the right technology at the right moment, performing an accurate analysis, making realistic goals and rightly forecasting the market behaviour. A deep research was conducted on companies that make a lot of investments in innovative technologies and those that ignore the role of technology in modern business. It was noticed that those that invested in technological research are always way ahead of the others in terms of profit, competitiveness or market share and overall success. The role of technology in business should not be ignored at any point because information technology is the only technology which fully provides you with the possibility of accurately analysing specific data and properly planning your business path. In addition, it also provides you with the necessary tools to solve and resolve all complex business problems that arise daily and pose distinguishing characteristics to the success of your business. Information technology actually eliminates the inconvenience of time-consumption and transportation costs involved in marketing your products or services. Having information technology in business is a sure way to reduce your inputs while optimizing outputs. With Information technology in business, you can market all your products or services in the global market either from home or office. This emphasizes the role of technology in modern business.

Describing methods, skills and resources needed to complete digital projects successfully

  • Planning

    • The plan of the project, what needs to be done to successfully complete the project.

    The old saying of “Failing to plan is planning to fail” still holds true. Without a plan your project is almost inevitably going to go off course and fail. So if you have a project then planning must be the first step, otherwise the team will have no point of reference when they encounter problems to refer to.

  • Task Delegation

    • The assignment of tasks throughout a team.

    Too often a leader of a team will take on board too much in the way of tasks and responsibility. This may be because they feel that it is expected of them, or that they don’t feel in control if all the eggs aren't in one basket so to speak. But the role of delegation can make everyones life much easier. It also gives team members a feeling of responsibility and trust. If a team leader isn’t delegating tasks too much pressure can also begin to build, leading to mistakes and frustration amongst the members. Delegation if vital to success and the leader must have confidence in his colleagues ability to be handed a job and complete without supervision.

  • Track Progress

    • Tracking progress is important.

    Information can be reviewed to make sure the plan is on track. The tracking of the progress of the project keeps all members in the loop. Issues can be resolved earlier rather than later when you may be too far down the rabbit hole so to speak to recover. It also gives everyone a mental picture of how things are progressing, are we ahead of schedule, are there things that need careful attention, what has or has not been done. It also shows if people are struggling or if there are things you could take on to re-leave someone else to get on with other tasks

  • Review

    • Reviews are important

    Reviews are needed to make sure that each part of the team are adequately and correctly implementing the correct procedures and polices. Regular reviews allowing individuals to give feedback outlining the progress of the project, members can become lost in the bigger picture of what is happening and feel alone an isolated. Reviews also give people the opportunity to raise issues or report issues that were raised at a previous review have been sorted or are still outstanding.

  • Record Results

    • Record Record Record

    The results of the project should be recorded for future reference or use. Keeping a record of the results are invaluable. It allows you to see where you went wrong and where you got it spot on. The same problem may arise in the future and records will allow you to look back and see how it was resolved last time. This can save valuable time and resources as everyone will have access to the results allowing you to build and improve your business as time marches on. Without recorded results your history of projects will leave your company as staff changes take place. You cannot just rely on memory, write it down, record it and file it away.

  • Task Delegation

    • No manager can do everything all the time, including you. This is where delegation comes in, but increasingly people find it difficult to allocate key tasks to staff effectively. Much like learning to ride a bike for the first time, or sitting on your first rollercoaster theme park ride, delegation requires managers to give up an element of their control. And if you’re a meticulous individual who likes everything to be ‘just right’ or perhaps a cautious manager who wants to impress senior executives, this can be scary thought. Then there’s the doubts that can enter a manager’s thinking; do I really want to take the time to explain this to someone else? Or would it just be faster to do it myself? I love doing the designs for the website, won't I miss it if I give it up? This is primarily because delegation in the workplace often involves giving your colleague the right to make decisions that are officially tied to your role and for which you are ultimately responsible. Typically, a lack of time and resources, or the desire to give someone else the opportunity to prove and improve their capabilities on a particular task, are the main reasons leaders delegate. But for managers used to making the same decisions and approach to tackling work duties, from preparing and delivering a key sales pitch to training a new recruit, they resist delegation, or, even, worse, fail to designate tasks appropriately – a recipe for failure. Recent research carried out by human resources development consultancy H2H shows that 35% of managers struggling with delegation feel unable to let go of control. Furthermore, 35% said they didn’t feel they had the necessary resources and 29% that it didn’t feel fair to ask someone else to do the task.

1.5 Describe how you would go about selecting and using IT systems and software – how do you select the right kind of technology for the job?

Gather Information

The best way to find out what the IT industry is currently using to do the best or most suitable job, is to speak with like-minded individuals that are actively involved within IT. This can be done by going to IT related workshops/business enterprise group meetings or reading up-to-date articles online from reliable IT industry reviewers and sources. Accurate and extensive research is key to finding out invaluable information on the subject and will direct the decision making process beyond this point. Sometimes demo or trial versions of digital software packages, for example, can be acquired easily enough from the Internet and tested out for potential suitability at no great risk or cost. Making certain that any installation of IT software is properly carried out before commencing the required job. Asking oneself if the, ‘IT set-up’ available to you is fit for purpose; if so continue ahead and if not, why not… can this easily and affordably be resolved? With so many front-end, back-end and full-stack developer systems available, it can often be difficult to know or select the right one for the job, but this can also be overcome by making sure enough technical experience and expertise is available to use the eventual type that is chosen for its relevant suitability.

1.7 Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects – How does GDPR impact digital projects?

Data Protection

Rules and Guidelines

GDPR now applies to all members of the EU as of 25th May 2018 GDPR is a broad range of protection for personal data. I gives a person control over how their data may be used and stored. Data is personal information such as: Your name, email address, religion, gender, location etc. It also gives an individual the right to see what information is held on them, and they may request that the data be deleted at any time. GDPR requires that the holder of the data store it in a responsible and secure manner, and that they inform the appropriate supervisory authority within 24 hours should a data breach become apparent. The risks for not following these measures are substantial as a company may be fined 20 million EURO or 4% of their global company income. Use of personal data is a very sensitive matter and great care must be taken not to use a persons data in a manner that may be unacceptable to them. For full guidelines see link below:
